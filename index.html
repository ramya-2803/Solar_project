Solar Declination (δ)

Formula: δ = 23.45° × sin(360°/365 × (284 + day_of_year))

Describes Earth's axial tilt relative to the sun throughout the year. Ranges from -23.45° (winter) to +23.45° (summer).

Incidence Angle (θ)

Formula: θ = |latitude - tilt - declination|

The angle between the sun's rays and the panel surface. Lower angles = better energy capture.

Tilted Irradiance (I_tilted)

Formula: I_tilted = I_horizontal × cos(θ)

Solar irradiance on a tilted surface. Accounts for the angle between rays and panel.

Cell Temperature (T_cell)

Formula: T_cell = T_ambient + (I_tilted / 800) × (NOCT - 20)

Panel temperature affects efficiency. Higher temp = lower efficiency. NOCT = 45°C (Nominal Operating Cell Temperature).

Temperature-Adjusted Efficiency (η_T)

Formula: η_T = η_ref × (1 - 0.0045 × (T_cell - 25))

Reference efficiency = 20%. Efficiency decreases ~0.45% per °C above 25°C.

Power Output (P)

Formula: P = η_T × I_use × A_panel, capped at inverter limit

Final AC power limited by inverter rating (350W). Peak efficiency at optimal tilt.

🆘 Help & Guide

How to Use This App

  1. Select a Date: Choose the date you want to optimize for (e.g., today, summer solstice, winter solstice).
  2. Set Your Location: Either allow geolocation, let the app auto-detect via IP, or click the map to manually select your latitude.
  3. Click Calculate: Watch the sunrise animation and see your results on the dashboard.
  4. View Results: See optimal tilt angle, expected power, panel temperature, and energy gain vs. fixed tilt.
  5. Export & Share: Download your report as PDF or share the results link.

Troubleshooting

  • GPS/Location not detected? Click "📍 Select from Map" and manually choose your location on the interactive map.
  • No results? Ensure you've entered a valid date and latitude is set (range: -90 to 90).
  • PDF download fails? Check your browser's download settings and try again.
  • Calculations seem off? Our ML model is trained on weather data. Different locations/dates may have varying prediction confidence.

Technical Details

Model: Machine learning (XGBoost/RandomForest) predicts horizontal irradiance from weather data.

Physics: Tilt angle optimization uses solar geometry (declination, incidence angle) and thermal effects (NOCT, temperature derate).

Data Source: Historical weather data (NASA/NOAA) + typical panel specifications.

❓ FAQ

Why does optimal tilt change daily?

The sun's position in the sky changes throughout the year due to Earth's axial tilt. The solar declination varies from -23.45° (winter solstice) to +23.45° (summer solstice). For maximum energy capture on a specific date, the panel must be oriented to minimize the angle between the sun's rays and the surface. Seasonal optimization can yield 15-20% more energy than a fixed tilt set to latitude.

How does ML improve accuracy?

Traditional models assume clear-sky irradiance. Real-world irradiance depends on cloud cover, humidity, and atmospheric conditions. Our ML model learns from historical weather data to predict horizontal irradiance more accurately. This feeds into the tilt optimization, yielding better real-world power estimates. The model inputs include temperature, humidity, wind speed, and seasonal factors.

What if GPS fails?

The app attempts geolocation in this order:

  1. Browser Geolocation API (requires your permission)
  2. IP-based lookup (ipapi.co)
  3. Manual map selection (click "📍 Select from Map")

If all fail, you can always manually enter your latitude in the input field or click on the map.

Why does temperature reduce efficiency?

Solar panels lose efficiency at higher temperatures due to the bandgap reduction effect. For typical silicon panels, efficiency decreases by ~0.45% per °C above 25°C. Our model calculates cell temperature using irradiance and ambient conditions, then applies a temperature derate. On hot days, the optimal tilt may differ slightly to balance maximum irradiance with thermal losses.

What is "Energy Gain vs Fixed Tilt"?

Fixed tilt systems are angled permanently at latitude (a simple rule of thumb). Energy gain shows the percentage improvement by using the optimal tilt for the chosen date. For example, a 25% gain means optimizing the tilt yields 25% more power than leaving it at a fixed latitude angle.

Can I use this for real installations?

This tool is designed for educational and estimating purposes. For production systems, consult professional solar engineers. Factors like roof slope, shading, structural limits, and local permits are critical and not modeled here.
